My story

Ideas have always been my thing. For as long as I can remember I've been flooded with them, on every subject imaginable, always chasing a fix for some problem I'd dreamed up in my own head.

These days my son comes to me with ideas about things he doesn't even know yet, and watching him is the clearest mirror of what I love most: taking a spark and, together with someone, making it real. In the past two years, with agents too.

My journey always started in marketing. I've always thought of it as the real bible of how the world works. Everything we know is wrapped in a story, and a good story is what carries an idea from one person into someone else's ears, on the way to a goal.

I knew early on that I'd either work for myself or be the entrepreneur inside someone else's company. That rhythm of sprinting forward, then circling back to run it again and better, was and still is simply how I'm built.

For me every business, from the tiny corner shop to the giant, is fascinating and full of its own puzzles. I get involved in projects from very different fields, and over the years I've touched a lot of different corners, from one-person operations to businesses with dozens of employees. That range isn't scatter, it's pattern recognition. The projects below are where that turned into something real.

Suricata · Education · Marketing systems Strategy & systems

Kfar Silver, a marketing system for a youth village and boarding school

The challenge

Kfar Silver is a youth village and boarding school. Competition between youth villages keeps growing, so the marketing has to be as efficient as it gets. But you can't outsource the enrollment conversation itself, educational staff have to be part of it.

What I built

A full marketing system: landing pages, ads that adapt, and an info bot that gives a student or parent what they need on the spot and keeps them close to us. Paired with a fast phone setup, every lead gets a response in under six hours. We're in our fifth year together, and we hit the targets every year.

This year

The goal was to take pressure off the sales team and convert more precisely, with a new set of questions. I used Google's Antigravity to test the system, plus a WhatsApp bot and a handful of automations.

Agritech · BD · Marketing · Sales Managing Partner

Pacífico Bambu, the business behind a bamboo venture

The project

Part of how I keep my thinking sharp is learning from fields that have nothing to do with each other. So I came on as a partner in a bamboo venture in Oaxaca, one that's about to harvest its first crop this year. The goal is something that doesn't exist in Mexico yet: full control of the bamboo supply chain, owning the local market first and then exporting to the US.

What I did

I took it over with some of the planting already in the ground, but no direction and no real plan. Within about a month I'd built a proper structure with two local partners: clear goals, an operational network, competitor and customer research, a CRM with real customer data, and a local go-to-market strategy. Today I run the marketing, the sales, building out the export, and bringing in investors, plus the day-to-day operation, alongside those two local partners.

The long game

This is a long-run bet, measured ten years out. For me it's also the opposite of all my AI work: hands in the soil, on farming that's healthy and good for the world. That's a balance I need now, and one I'm sure I'll keep needing.

Retail tech · AI Agents · Product Founder & builder

TiendaBot, business intelligence for Mexico's corner shops

The problem

Mexico has more than a million corner shops. Their tech runs anywhere from a paper notebook to a PC that just stores numbers, with no API and no real way to analyze anything. Mostly because proper tools are expensive and the technical base simply isn't there.

What I built

A conversation with a local friend sent me into research, and then into building him a solution: a business-intelligence app for the shop. Agents run the different areas and bring every number into one place, with an orchestrator agent that takes any question and answers it from the data. The hard part was parsing data locked in an old, outdated format on the shop's computer and turning it into an automation that lands every morning with everything the owner needs.

The result

From a friend's problem to a working BI app that parses legacy POS data, live in one pilot shop and expanding to a second. The goal is an off-the-shelf, phone-only product that lets any shop owner manage inventory, suppliers, invoices and products from their phone, at a price that actually works for them.

Marketplaces · Product 0→1 · Emerging markets Founder & builder

ChambaLocal & ColectiVa, small apps for real towns in Oaxaca

The challenge

On the Oaxaca coast, workers and employers had no good way to find each other, and the people who ride the shared colectivos had no idea when the next one was coming. In both cases the real problem was trust, not technology.

What I built

ChambaLocal is a Spanish-first jobs marketplace for the coast, built as a web app with a Telegram bot, with double opt-in matching so nobody gets spammed. ColectiVa is a shared-transport app I'm keeping gentle on purpose: a first version that only ever says helpful things, so drivers learn to trust it before it does anything harder.

Where it stands

ChambaBot is in a live trial right now, in final testing before we start advertising and marketing. ColectiVa is still in mockups, in progress with a first design partner.
